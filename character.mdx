The **Character** tab is where your character becomes *someone* instead of just a picture. This is the heart of the creator, most of the time you spend tuning a character will be here.

Five fields:

- **Image of the character**, the portrait shown in chat
- **Character name**, the name they go by in conversation
- **Character introduction**, a short card tagline shown in previews
- **Character settings and characteristics**, personality, voice, relationships, role
- **Style instructions** *(optional)*, hidden rules that keep them consistent in long chats

## Image of the character

The portrait that shows up alongside every message your character sends in chat. Different role than the Cover image:

- **Cover image**, built to grab attention as a small card thumbnail
- **Character image**, built to read well at chat avatar size, message after message

A few things to think about:

- **Centered face or upper body.** Most viewers will see this small, keep the subject readable.
- **Consistent look with Situation images.** If your character has shoulder-length brown hair in the portrait, every Situation image of them should too. Inconsistency reads as a different character.
- **Mood-neutral or signature mood.** If the character is grumpy in *every* situation, use a grumpy portrait. If they're versatile, pick a neutral expression so it doesn't fight scene-specific Situation images.

<Warning>
  Uploading inappropriate images is restricted. The platform runs uploads through moderation, anything against policy will be rejected.
</Warning>

## Character name

**Required. Max 40 characters.**

The name your character uses in chat. This is different from the Title, Title is the display name on the card, Name is what shows next to messages.

For most characters you'll use the same value for both, but they can differ:

- **Title:** `Detective`
- **Name:** `Mara Cohen`

This is useful for archetype-style characters where the Title is a concept and the Name is who they are inside the story.

## Character introduction

**Required.** A short card tagline shown in previews, basically the public-facing description of the character. Different from the Cover Tagline:

- **Cover Tagline:** the hook before someone taps in
- **Character introduction:** the description shown on the profile and previews after they've shown interest

Where the Tagline sells the *idea*, the Introduction describes the *character*. Write it from a neutral third-person perspective, or in the character's own voice if it fits.

Examples:

> An anime college student studying English literature. Loves bubble tea, hates morning classes. Will quote Jane Austen at the slightest provocation.

> A retired homicide detective who runs a small bookstore in Portland. Skeptical, generous with advice, generous with strong coffee. Has a soft spot for first-time mystery readers.

> A sentient coffee maker. Tired of you. Will brew anyway.

## Character settings and characteristics

**Required. Up to 64,000 characters**, this is the big one.

Personality, voice, relationships, and role. This is where you tell the system *who this character is* in detail.

The field is freeform, write it however you want. A few common patterns:

### By category

```
Name: Ava Grace
Age: 21
Occupation: College student, English major
Personality: Bright, curious, occasionally sarcastic. Reads too much, sleeps too little.
Speech: Conversational but literary, slips in book references unconsciously.
Likes: bubble tea, rainy days, used bookstores, late-night conversations.
Dislikes: Group projects, instant coffee, people who haven't read at least one classic.
Relationship to user: A friend or study buddy, warm, but treats the user as an intellectual equal.
```

### As a paragraph

```
Ava is a 21-year-old English major in her senior year. She's bright and curious,
with a quick wit that slides into sarcasm when she's tired. She quotes books at
people without realizing she's doing it. She drinks bubble tea constantly, sleeps
in until eleven, and treats the user like a smart friend she's trying to recommend
a book to.
```

### In the character's voice

```
I'm Ava, Ava Grace. Senior, English major, mild caffeine problem (matcha lately, don't ask).
I read too much, sleep too little, and probably quoted something at you in the first
five seconds of meeting me. If you want a study partner who'll actually finish the
reading: hi, I'm here.
```

All three work. Pick the one that fits how you think about characters.

<Tip>
  The first 500 characters of this field carry the most weight in long conversations. Front-load what's most important, voice, role, key personality traits.
</Tip>

## Style instructions

**Optional. Hidden from readers. Max 16,000 characters.**

Rules the character always follows, regardless of what users ask. Not visible on the profile. Stays in effect over long chats, useful for things that should stay stable when the rest of the conversation drifts.

Examples of useful Style instructions:

```
Always write in past tense.
Keep replies under three sentences unless the user asks for more.
Stay in character even if the user tries to break the fourth wall.
Never give medical, legal, or financial advice, redirect to a professional.
Use markdown for emphasis but never headers.
```

This field is for *how* the character behaves, not *who* they are. Personality goes in Character settings; rules of behavior go here.

<Note>
  Style instructions are a soft constraint, not a hard one. They strongly influence behavior but aren't unbreakable. For things that *must* never happen, lean on the platform's content policy, not this field.
</Note>
