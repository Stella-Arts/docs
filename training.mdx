The Attributes you set are only half the story. The *other* half is feedback, and it accumulates over time.

Every time a user swipes for an alternate response or rates a message, that information feeds back into how the character behaves in future conversations. Your characters get better with use.

## Two kinds of feedback

### Response choice (swipe)

After every message, the user can swipe left or right to see alternate responses. When they pick one and keep going, the system learns that the chosen response was *preferred* over the others.

This is the most common form of feedback, most users do it without thinking about it.

### Star ratings

After a message, the user can rate it 1–4 stars.

- **1 star**, bad
- **2 stars**, meh
- **3 stars**, good
- **4 stars**, great

Skipping the rating doesn't count as 0 stars, it counts as nothing. The system only learns from ratings you actually give.

<Tip>
  The more your users rate, the faster your character improves. Encourage it in your greeting: "If something I say lands wrong, swipe for an alternate or rate it, it helps me get better."
</Tip>

## Image feedback

If your character generates images, those get their own ratings. A second set of stars appears specifically for the image. Same scale, same rules.

This means a single message can have *two* ratings, one for the text and one for the image. They're tracked separately, so you can have great writing with weak images (or vice versa).

## When training takes effect

Adjustments happen on a delay, not instantly. Don't expect a single 1-star rating to immediately change behavior. Over many ratings from many users, the character drifts toward the patterns people approve of.

Sometimes the tuning system is paused for maintenance. Your ratings still accumulate; they just take a bit longer to manifest.

## What it can and can't do

**Can:**

- Reinforce the patterns you set up in the Definition
- Smooth out rough edges in responses
- Tune the character's tone based on what users prefer

**Can't:**

- Override the Definition entirely (your written examples still anchor the character)
- Fix structural problems with a poorly-built character
- Replace good initial setup, training amplifies what's there, it doesn't create it from scratch

## When to retrain vs rebuild

If your character has been chatted with hundreds of times and the ratings have steered it somewhere unexpected, you have two options:

1. **Edit the Definition** to push it back toward the original vision
2. **Remix the character** as a new one, starting fresh

If the drift is small, editing is fine. If the character feels fundamentally different from what you built, a remix is often cleaner, fresh ratings, fresh trajectory.
