Sutorichat is powerful, but it isn't magic. Some things are still hard. Here are the most common ones and how to work around them.

<Note>
  This list reflects the system as it stands today. The model improves over time, and what's a limitation now may not be next month. When something doesn't work the way you expect, try again in a few weeks.
</Note>

## Text challenges

### Memory

As a conversation grows, the character can forget details from earlier turns, especially things that weren't repeated. If something is important, mention it again.

**Workaround:** put critical facts about the character (backstory, setting, rules) into the Definition, not just into the opening greeting. The Definition stays in context longer than a single user message.

### Factuality

Characters make things up. Confidently. If your character recommends a real book, real medication, or real legal advice, double-check it. Don't trust it as a source.

**Workaround:** for characters where factual accuracy matters (tutors, advisors), include a note in the greeting like "I may get details wrong, verify anything important."

### Drift under pressure

Even with careful Negative Guidance, a determined user can sometimes push a character out of its lane. The system is designed to be cooperative, which means it tends to follow the user's lead.

**Workaround:** more example dialogs in the Definition showing the character *recovering* from off-topic prompts. Reinforce the desired behavior with multiple examples.

### Tone consistency

A character can wobble in tone across a long conversation, getting more formal, more casual, more verbose, depending on the user.

**Workaround:** in example dialogs, show the character maintaining their tone across different user styles.

### Length consistency

Characters often default to slightly verbose responses. If you want a terse character, demonstrate it explicitly in the Definition, multiple example responses that are 1–2 sentences max.

## Image challenges

### Consistency across images

The single biggest image issue. The same character can look different from image to image, different hair, eyes, age, outfit. See the [Consistency](/images/consistency) page for the standard workaround (a verbatim anchor phrase).

### Detail quality

Fine details, fingers, complex jewelry, text on signs, exact tattoos, are unreliable. Models still struggle here.

**Workaround:** avoid asking for tight detail close-ups. Medium shots and wider tend to look much better.

### Pop culture rendering

The system's visual vocabulary doesn't always match its textual vocabulary. You can chat with a character who clearly knows a movie, but image generation might not be able to depict its specific characters or visual style accurately.

**Workaround:** describe characters by their *visual traits* rather than by name. Instead of "Sherlock Holmes," try "a tall lean man in a deerstalker hat, holding a pipe, Victorian London."

### Style mixing

Putting too many styles in one prompt (`oil painting anime watercolor photograph`) produces muddy results. Pick one primary style.

### Text in images

Words on signs, book titles, labels, usually unreadable or wrong. If text matters, design around it, not into it.

## Platform-level moderation

Some content is filtered platform-wide. This is non-negotiable and not something you can work around in the Definition or Image Style.

If a generated image is rejected or a response is sanitized, it's usually a moderation pass, not a creative failure. Adjust the request to fit the policy.

## What to do when something seems broken

1. **Wait and try again.** A single bad output isn't necessarily a pattern, try swiping for an alternate.
2. **Edit the Definition.** Add an example that shows the *right* behavior in the same situation that's failing.
3. **Simplify.** Long, complicated prompts often produce worse results than short, focused ones.
4. **Report it.** If a character is doing something seriously wrong, going off the rails, ignoring safety, producing harmful content, use the Report button. Even if the Definition is private, your report gets reviewed.

<Tip>
  Don't fight the system. If something keeps failing the same way after three tries, change your approach instead of repeating it harder. The shape of the prompt is usually the issue.
</Tip>
