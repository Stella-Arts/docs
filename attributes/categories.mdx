**Optional.** Up to 3 categories per character.

Categories don't change how your character behaves. They affect how it shows up in search and browse, and how new users find it.

## Picking categories

When you create a character, you'll see a list of around 50 categories. Pick the ones that *actually* describe the character, not the ones that are most popular.

- A wizard who answers questions → `Fantasy`, `Tutor`, `Roleplay`
- A grumpy refrigerator → `Comedy`, `Objects`
- A Spanish tutor → `Education`, `Languages`, `Tutor`
- A horror narrator → `Horror`, `Storytelling`, `Roleplay`

If your character fits more than three categories, pick the three that make discovery most useful, usually the most specific ones, not the most generic.

## What makes categories worth picking accurately

Other users browse by category. If you tag your `Friendly Chef` as `Horror` because it's a more popular category, the people who find your character are the wrong audience. They'll bounce, and your character won't grow.

<Tip>
  Look at characters on the home page that feel similar to yours. Check their categories. That's your shortlist.
</Tip>
