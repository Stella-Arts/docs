There are two separate visibility settings: who can **chat** with the character, and who can **see** the Definition. They're independent.

## Character Visibility

Controls who can start a conversation with your character.

| Setting | Who can chat | Where it appears |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Public** | Anyone | Home page, search, browse |
| **Unlisted** | Anyone with the link | Nowhere, link-only |
| **Private** | Only you | Nowhere |

Most people set new characters to **Unlisted** while they're tuning them, then flip to **Public** when they're happy.

<Warning>
  If you share an Unlisted link publicly (on Twitter, Reddit, anywhere), anyone who finds the link can chat. Unlisted is "link-only", it isn't truly private.
</Warning>

## Definition Visibility & Remixing

The Definition is the big free-form field where you write example dialogs and behavior notes. You can decide whether other users can see it.

Three settings:

- **Public & Remixable**, anyone can see the Definition and clone it as a starting point for their own character.
- **Public & Viewable**, others can read the Definition but can't remix.
- **Private Definition**, the Definition is hidden, even from people who chat with the character.

You can still **chat** with a character that has a private Definition, the privacy only hides the Definition field itself. Name, greeting, descriptions, and image style remain visible if the character is Public or Unlisted.

## When to make the Definition private

Reasonable reasons:

- You spent a lot of time on a clever pattern and don't want it copied wholesale
- The Definition contains spoilers (a mystery game, a puzzle character)
- You're still tuning and don't want others to copy a half-finished version

Reasons that usually backfire:

- "I don't want anyone to learn from it", Sutorichat has a strong remix culture. Sharing how you built your character usually grows your audience.
- "I'm worried someone will steal it", characters can be remixed, but yours stays put. Remixes are credited to the original.

<Tip>
  When in doubt, leave it Public & Remixable. You'll learn from other people's remixes too.
</Tip>
