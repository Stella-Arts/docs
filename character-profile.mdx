Every character has a public profile: a card that shows their name, avatar, short description, and how many people have chatted with them. It's the storefront for your character.

## What appears on the profile

- **Name** and **avatar**
- **Short description**, the one-line hook
- **Long description**, written in the character's own voice
- **Categories**, tags that help discovery
- **Suggested Starters**, clickable prompts so new users know how to begin
- **Example Conversations**, sample exchanges showing the character in action
- **Interaction count** and **remix count**

If you make your Definition viewable, others can also click through to see exactly how the character was built, useful for learning, copying, and remixing.

## Finding your character's settings

Two ways to get to the editor:

1. From your profile page, tap the **gear icon** next to the character's avatar.
2. While chatting with a character you created, open the menu in the top-right and choose **View Character Settings**.

<Note>
  If you don't see a gear or a settings option, the creator has marked the Definition as private. That's their choice, you can still chat, but you can't peek at how they built it.
</Note>

## Sharing your character

The profile has a share button that copies a direct link. Anyone with the link can chat with the character if visibility is set to **Public** or **Unlisted**. Private characters can't be shared.

<Warning>
  If your character does something unexpected or harmful and you can't see the Definition, you can report it from the profile menu.
</Warning>
