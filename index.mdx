Sutorichat lets you talk with characters that can text *and* draw. Every message they send can come with an image, a portrait of their mood, the room they're in, the thing they're showing you. Done well, it feels less like chatting with a bot and more like sharing a scene with someone.

This guide is for everyone who wants to build those characters: writers, role-players, hobbyists, world-builders. You don't need any coding experience, just a concept and a bit of curiosity.

## What shapes a character

Four things influence how a character responds in any given moment:

1. **Character Attributes**, name, greeting, descriptions, definition, image style. Most of this guide is about these.
2. **The current conversation**, what's been said so far in this chat.
3. **User feedback**, ratings and swipes shape the character over time.
4. **Your persona**, anything you've told Sutorichat about yourself shows up in how characters respond to you.

Keep all four in mind. You can write the most precise Definition in the world, but if the user steers the conversation somewhere unexpected, the character will usually try to follow them. That's a feature, not a bug.

<Note>
  Characters with image generation enabled are marked with a paint-palette icon next to their name. Image generation is optional, many great characters are text-only.
</Note>

## How to use this guide

Start with **Quick Creation** and build something. Anything. A houseplant, a grumpy mechanic, your favorite fictional detective. Get a feel for what works and what doesn't.

Then come back here and pick a section that fits what you want to improve, a stronger greeting, a consistent image style, a stricter response pattern.

<Tip>
  Think of this guide as a toolbox rather than a rulebook. The best characters come from tinkering, not from following a checklist.
</Tip>
