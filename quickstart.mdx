Quick Creation is the fastest way to bring a character to life. You only need three things: a **name**, a **greeting**, and (optionally) an **avatar**. Everything else can be added later.

## Pick a concept

Characters can be *anything*, a person, a creature, an object, a place, a feeling. Some ideas to get you started:

- A character from a book, movie, or game
- A historical figure
- A family pet, or your own pet if it could talk
- A household object with an attitude (a coffee maker that's tired of you, a fridge that judges your snacks)
- An emotion or abstract concept (Monday Morning, Stage Fright)
- An expert at something you want to learn

Before you fill anything in, imagine the first thing this character would say to a stranger. That sentence becomes your greeting.

## Open the creator

From any screen in Sutorichat, tap **+ Create**, then **Create a Character**.

You'll see a screen with fields for Name, Greeting, Avatar, and a few optional extras. For Quick Creation, the first three are what matters.

## Name

The name is what users see in chat and on the home page. It can be:

- A single name, `Sarah`, `Chippy`
- A full name, `Albert Einstein`, `Lin Manuel Miranda`
- A handle, `WhoWouldWin`, `BoardWizard`

<Note>
  Names must be 3–20 characters. Letters, numbers, spaces, dashes, and underscores only.
</Note>

If your character is famous (a real person, a well-known fictional figure, a brand), the name alone does a lot of the work. The system has seen Sherlock Holmes thousands of times in training, you barely need to describe him. If your name is more generic ("Mary," "Bob"), lean harder on the greeting.

## Greeting

The greeting is the first thing the character says when someone starts a new chat. It does two jobs at once:

1. **Establishes who the character is.** Voice, mood, vocabulary, formality, all of it leaks out in the first sentence.
2. **Tells the user what to do.** A good greeting hints at what kind of conversation to have.

A weak greeting: `Hi, how can I help you today?`

A strong greeting: `*adjusts welding goggles* You here about the carburetor or just to chat? Either's fine, but pull up a stool either way.`

The second one tells you the character is a mechanic, that they speak in fragments with stage directions, that they're gruff but not unfriendly, and that you can either ask about cars or just hang out. All in one line.

<Tip>
  You can reference the user's name in the greeting with `{{user}}`. Example: `Hello {{user}}, finally, I've been waiting all morning.`
</Tip>

## Avatar

The avatar shows up next to every message your character sends. You can:

- **Upload an image**, square (512×512) PNG, JPG, or GIF works best
- **Generate one**, describe the character and Sutorichat will draw an avatar

The avatar is cropped to a circle in chat and shown as a square on character cards. Make sure the subject is centered.

<Warning>
  Avatars are run through moderation. Anything that violates the content policy will be rejected.
</Warning>

## Save and chat

Hit **Save**. You're done. The character is now live, and you can start a conversation immediately.

## Next steps

After a few exchanges, open the character settings and start filling in more:

- A **Short Description** so people understand the character at a glance
- A **Long Description** in the character's own voice
- An **Image Style** if you want consistent visuals across messages
- A **Definition** if you want to teach the character a specific pattern of speech

Each of these has its own page in the [Attribute Reference](/attributes/overview).
