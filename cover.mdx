The **Cover** tab is the storefront for your character. It's the first thing other users see, the picture they tap, the title they read, the tagline that decides whether they start a chat or scroll past.

Three fields and one toggle:

- **Cover image**, the square image shown on the character card
- **Title**, the character's display name
- **Tagline**, the one-line hook below the title
- **Make Private**, toggle off to share publicly (default), on to keep it just for you

## Cover image

The Cover image is the picture shown on the character card on the home page, in search, and on the character profile. You can:

- **Generate / Edit Image**, describe what you want and Sutorichat creates it for you
- **Upload Image**, bring your own square image

A good cover image has three qualities:

1. **A clear subject.** Anyone scrolling should see your character at a glance.
2. **Distinct from other characters.** If you build the tenth "anime girl" of the day, what makes yours different visually? Lean into specifics, the outfit, the setting, the mood, the lighting.
3. **Matches the tagline.** If the tagline says "smart college grad," the image shouldn't show a child or a fantasy warrior. The two together should *tell the same story*.

<Tip>
  The Cover image and the Character image (next tab) can be the same picture or different. Use different ones when the Cover should be eye-catching at thumbnail size, and the Character image should work full-resolution alongside chat messages.
</Tip>

## Title

**Max 60 characters.**

The Title is the public display name. It appears under the cover image on the home page and at the top of every chat.

Some patterns that work:

- **Simple name**, `Anime Girl`, `Ava Grace`, `Marcus`
- **Name + identity**, `Marcus, Stoic Philosopher`, `Mei, Tea Shop Owner`
- **Concept**, `Late Night DJ`, `Grumpy Bookseller`

If your character is *anchored to a specific persona* (a named original character, a famous figure), use the actual name. If it's a *role-based concept*, the role can be the title.

<Note>
  The Title doesn't have to be unique, many characters can share a name. Sutorichat distinguishes them by creator.
</Note>

## Tagline

A one-line hook that appears below the Title. Think of it as the elevator pitch, a tiny line that makes someone curious enough to tap in.

Good patterns:

- **Identity**, `Smart anime college grad`, `Retired homicide detective`, `Sentient coffee maker`
- **Premise**, `Plays the alphabet improv game with you`, `Recommends your next favorite novel`
- **Vibe**, `Soft-spoken, brutally honest, will give you life advice over tea`

Avoid:

- Spammy hooks (`The BEST anime girl on Sutorichat!!!`)
- Repeating the title (`Anime Girl is an anime girl`)
- Being too vague (`A friend.`)

A great tagline does what advertising copy does: tells the reader *exactly* what they'd get out of starting this chat, in a voice that already hints at the character.

## Make Private

Off by default. Public characters appear on the home page and in search. Private characters are only visible to you, useful when you're still building and don't want strangers chatting with a half-finished version.

<Tip>
  Toggle Make Private on while you're tuning the character. Once it feels right, toggle it back off to share publicly.
</Tip>
