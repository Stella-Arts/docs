**Optional.** Up to 100 characters.

Image Style is a short phrase that gets blended into *every* image your character generates. Use it to establish a baseline look, the visual signature of the character, so individual images don't have to repeat the same style details over and over.

## Where it lives

Image Style is a separate field in the character editor, right under the toggle for Image Generation. You don't put it in the Definition.

## What to put there

Short, visual, and stylistic. Don't describe the *subject*, that comes per-message. Describe the *look*.

Good examples:

- `Ghibli-inspired watercolor, soft pastels, hand-drawn linework`
- `1980s anime cel art, bright saturated colors, retro grain`
- `Dark fantasy oil painting, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting`
- `Pixel art, 16-bit era, limited color palette`
- `Photorealistic cinematic, shallow depth of field, film grain`
- `Children's storybook illustration, soft gouache, warm earth tones`

## How it combines with each message

If your Image Style is:

```
Watercolor illustration, soft pastels, hand-drawn lines
```

And your character writes:

```
*A cat sitting on a windowsill, looking at falling snow*
```

The actual prompt the model uses becomes something like:

```
A cat sitting on a windowsill, looking at falling snow,
watercolor illustration, soft pastels, hand-drawn lines
```

Your character can focus on writing scene descriptions. The visual style stays consistent automatically.

## When to leave it empty

If your character's *content* varies wildly in style, a photographer character who can shoot anything from a portrait to a sports moment, leave the field empty and let each message specify its own style.

## How specific should it be?

A trade-off:

- **Very specific** (`Anime, Studio Ghibli, watercolor backgrounds, 1990s grain, soft pastels, bokeh, atmospheric`), strong consistency, but limits what scenes look right
- **Moderately specific** (`Anime, watercolor, soft pastels`), good consistency with flexibility
- **Very loose** (`Anime`), barely constrains anything, useful as a baseline

Most successful image-generating characters land in the middle. Three to six well-chosen words is usually enough.

## A pattern that works: pick a reference

If you have a specific look in mind, write down the *category, medium, and era* of the inspiration:

- **Category:** anime, photography, oil painting, watercolor, pixel art, 3D render
- **Medium:** what would it be drawn or shot on? Pencil sketch, gouache, 35mm film, ink wash?
- **Era / tradition:** 80s anime, golden-age comics, art-nouveau, modern indie game?

`80s anime, hand-drawn cel, ink wash backgrounds` is far more specific, and more reproducible, than `anime style`.

## Reference: prompt books

Two prompt collections that are good starting points for vocabulary, even though they're written for general image models:

- The OpenArt Prompt Book
- The DALL·E 2 Prompt Book

Both list dozens of style words with example renderings, which is great for vocabulary-building.

<Tip>
  Once you settle on a style, **save the exact phrase** so you can reuse it on related characters. A recurring style ties a "universe" of characters together visually.
</Tip>
