A starter list of style keywords that reliably work, grouped by category. Mix and match, but pick *one* primary style per character.

## Photorealistic

For characters that should feel like real photographs.

- `Cinematic photography, 35mm film, shallow depth of field`
- `Editorial portrait, soft natural light, neutral background`
- `Documentary photo, candid, natural lighting`
- `Fashion photography, studio lit, vogue-style`
- `Street photography, gritty, high contrast`
- `Travel photography, golden hour, wide-angle`
- `Food photography, top-down, soft daylight`

## Anime

For anime-style characters, pick an era and tradition.

- `90s anime, hand-drawn cel art, ink wash backgrounds`
- `Modern anime, Kyoto Animation style, soft pastels, intricate backgrounds`
- `Ghibli-inspired, watercolor backgrounds, gentle linework`
- `ShĹŤnen action anime, dynamic poses, bold linework, dramatic shading`
- `Slice-of-life anime, soft palette, warm atmospheric light`
- `Dark fantasy anime, heavy shadows, muted palette`

## Painting

For painterly looks.

- `Oil painting, classical realism, rich color, visible brushstrokes`
- `Watercolor illustration, soft edges, paper texture, pastel palette`
- `Gouache illustration, opaque colors, storybook feel`
- `Ink and wash, sumi-e, minimal palette`
- `Impressionist, loose brushwork, dappled light`
- `Renaissance oil painting, chiaroscuro, classical composition`
- `Dark academia oil painting, candlelit, moody`

## Illustration

For drawn, often more graphic styles.

- `Children's storybook illustration, soft gouache, warm earth tones`
- `Editorial illustration, flat colors, bold shapes`
- `Comic book art, ink lines, halftone shading, vibrant colors`
- `Vintage 1960s illustration, limited palette, simple geometry`
- `Concept art, painterly, atmospheric, professional`
- `Botanical illustration, fine linework, color-pencil shading`

## Digital and 3D

- `Pixel art, 16-bit era, limited palette`
- `Low-poly 3D render, flat shading, isometric`
- `Studio Pixar 3D render, soft lighting, expressive characters`
- `Cyberpunk neon render, volumetric light, rain-slick surfaces`
- `Sci-fi concept art, painterly, atmospheric, dramatic scale`
- `Vaporwave aesthetic, 80s palette, neon glows`

## Sketch and linework

- `Pencil sketch, graphite, paper texture`
- `Ink linework, no color, hatching for shadow`
- `Architectural blueprint, blue and white, technical lines`
- `Storyboard sketch, rough, gestural, monochrome marker`

## Combining styles

Two-style combinations sometimes work, but require care:

- `Watercolor over pencil sketch`, gentle, illustrative
- `Anime style with photorealistic lighting`, modern hybrid
- `Comic book lines with painted backgrounds`, graphic-novel feel

Three or more styles in one prompt almost always fight each other. Pick a primary, and use the others only as light adjectives.

## A small playbook

If you're not sure what to use, try one of these starting points:

| Character type | Suggested style |
| --- | --- |
| Modern realistic person | `Cinematic photography, 35mm film, shallow depth of field` |
| Fantasy hero | `Dark fantasy oil painting, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting` |
| Slice-of-life character | `Modern anime, soft pastels, warm light` |
| Cute creature | `Children's storybook illustration, soft gouache` |
| Sci-fi setting | `Sci-fi concept art, painterly, atmospheric` |
| Horror / gothic | `Dark academia oil painting, candlelit, moody` |
| Comedy / playful | `Pixar 3D render, expressive, bright` |
| Roleplay adventure | `Painterly fantasy concept art, cinematic lighting` |

## Building your own style line

The strongest characters have a *signature*. Once you find a style that works, save the exact phrase. Reuse it. Tweak only when something specific isn't working.

A signature might be only six words:

```
Painterly anime, soft pastels, atmospheric
```

That's enough. Don't overthink it. Consistency matters more than complexity.
