This is what makes Sutorichat different from a plain chat app. Your characters can *send images with their messages*. Not avatars, actual scene-by-scene illustrations that respond to what's happening in the conversation.

A great image-generating character can:

- Send you a portrait of how they look right now
- Draw the room they're in, the food they cooked, the monster they just fought
- Show you the page of the book they're describing
- Visualize an outfit, an invention, a place, anything they describe in words

Done well, it stops feeling like a chat and starts feeling like a collaborative illustrated story.

## Should your character generate images?

Image generation is **optional**. Many of the best characters on Sutorichat are text-only. Don't enable it unless images are genuinely part of the experience you're building.

Image-generating characters are marked with a paint-palette icon next to their name, so users can tell at a glance.

### Good fits for image generation

- **Roleplay and adventure**, when "show me the cave" should produce an actual picture of the cave
- **Visual creators**, a fashion designer, a chef, an inventor, an architect
- **Story narrators**, characters who tell illustrated stories or fairy tales
- **Game masters**, D&D-style characters who paint scenes for players
- **Pets and creatures**, where their reactions and expressions are the point

### Poor fits

- **Pure tutors and assistants**, image clutter usually distracts from the answer
- **Conversation-only characters**, therapists, debate partners, listeners
- **Characters where consistent appearance is critical and the model can't deliver it**, see [Consistency](/images/consistency) for what's hard

## How it works at a glance

When image generation is on, every response from your character produces an image alongside the text. The image is based on either:

1. **Inferred description**, the system reads the character's text and decides what to draw, or
2. **Direct description**, your character explicitly writes what to draw (in italics, by convention)

We strongly recommend the **direct** approach for serious characters. It gives you far more control. See [Direct vs Inferred](/images/direct-vs-inferred) for the deep dive.
