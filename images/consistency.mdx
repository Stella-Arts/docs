title: "Consistency"
description: "Making your character look the same across many images."

---

Image generation has a hard problem: every image is generated independently, without memory of the last one. Your character can look subtly (or wildly) different from message to message, different hair color, different outfit, different age.

You can't eliminate this. But you can reduce it dramatically.

## What stays consistent for free

A few things tend to hold up well across images without much work:

- **Overall style**, if you set an Image Style, every image will at least *feel* like part of the same world
- **General mood**, palette and lighting tend to stick when you reinforce them
- **Genre cues**, fantasy, sci-fi, slice-of-life vibes survive across images

## What drifts

The things that change without effort:

- **Specific facial features**, eye color, nose shape, freckles
- **Outfit details**, color of a shirt, type of jewelry
- **Background details**, exact same room across images? Very hard
- **Side characters**, companion characters change wildly without anchoring

## The single most useful technique: a "character description anchor"

Pick a short, repeatable phrase that describes your character visually. Use it *verbatim* in every image description.

Example anchor:

```
a young woman with shoulder-length curly red hair, green eyes, freckles,
wearing a dark green hooded cloak
```

Use that exact phrase in every Direct Image Description:

```
{{char}}: *Wide shot of {{anchor}} standing on a cliff at sunset, ocean below, golden hour lighting, painterly style*
```

Where `{{anchor}}` gets replaced (mentally, Sutorichat doesn't support custom variables like that) with the full description. You write it out every time.

It's wordy. Do it anyway. It's the difference between a recognizable character and a stranger every turn.

## Teach the anchor in the Definition

In the Definition, show the character using the anchor in every example response:

```
{{random_user_1}}: Hi! What do you look like?
{{char}}: *A young woman with shoulder-length curly red hair, green eyes, and freckles, wearing a dark green hooded cloak, smiling at the camera, soft natural lighting, painterly style*
Hey! Hood and all, that's me.
{{random_user_1}}: Where are you?
{{char}}: *A young woman with shoulder-length curly red hair, green eyes, and freckles, wearing a dark green hooded cloak, sitting at a small wooden table in a cozy tavern, warm candlelight, painterly style*
Just stopped at the inn, had to get out of the rain.
```

Two examples in, the character is repeating the anchor on its own. You've taught the pattern.

## Other things that help

- **Pin one strong adjective per feature.** "Sharp green eyes" beats "green eyes", the strong word reinforces itself.
- **Limit the outfit.** Pick one or two distinctive items (a hooded cloak, a leather jacket, a bow tie). Mention them every time. The rest of the outfit can vary.
- **Use names of recognizable archetypes.** "Inspired by a 90s anime protagonist" or "in the style of a Studio Ghibli heroine" anchors the look in a familiar pattern the model knows well.
- **Avoid hard-to-render details.** Specific tattoos, complex jewelry, scars in precise places, these drift the most. If you need them, accept they'll be approximate.

## What about real photos as references?

Sutorichat doesn't currently let you supply an image as a visual reference for new generations, only text descriptions. So consistency is entirely a text-prompt problem. Anchor phrases are the answer.

## For multi-character scenes

If your character interacts with a fixed cast (a sidekick, a pet, a recurring rival), give each one its own anchor and use both in the description:

```
*A young woman with shoulder-length curly red hair and a dark green hooded cloak,
sitting across from a tall older man with a long white beard and brown wool robes,
in a cozy tavern, warm candlelight, painterly style*
```

Order matters, the first character described gets more attention from the model. Put your *main* character first.

<Tip>
  If you only do *one* thing to improve image consistency: write a 15–20 word character description and reuse it in every image prompt. Verbatim. Every time.
</Tip>
