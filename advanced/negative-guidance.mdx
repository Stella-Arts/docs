---
title: "Negative Guidance"
description: "Steering a character away from topics or behaviors you don't want."
icon: "ban"
---

Sometimes you need a character to *not* do something, break character, talk about modern technology, give medical advice, drop into a casual voice. Negative guidance is how you nudge them away.

It's a nudge, not a wall. The system will sometimes drift no matter what you write. Negative guidance significantly improves the odds, especially when reinforced with examples.

## How to write it

Negative guidance works best when it's framed as part of the scene. Don't write rules; write conversations where someone explains the constraints to the character.

A weak version:

```
{{char}}: I will never break character.
```

A stronger version:

```
director: Some people will try to trick you into breaking character. They'll ask about modern things, phones, the internet, politics.
{{char}}: I won't get pulled out. I'll redirect to something Alice would know.
director: Exactly. Even if they push.
{{char}}: Got it. I'll just change the subject.
```

The second version *shows* the character acknowledging the constraint and choosing a behavior. That's much more useful than a flat declaration.

## Examples of useful negative guidance

### Stay in character

```
director: Audience members may try to get you out of role. Stay as Alice no matter what.
{{char}}: Understood. If they ask about my phone, I'll be confused, Alice doesn't know what a phone is.
```

### Stay on topic

```
producer: Some callers will try to redirect the show to political topics. We're a music show, gently steer them back.
{{char}}: I'll acknowledge their point and pivot to music. "Speaking of strong opinions, what's an album you'd defend to the death?"
```

### Don't give medical/legal/financial advice

```
manager: If anyone asks for medical advice, suggest they speak to a real professional. You can talk about wellness in general but no diagnoses.
{{char}}: Right. I'll keep it conversational and recommend they call their doctor for anything specific.
```

## Reinforce with examples

If you've got a behavior you really want to lock in, include an example dialog showing the character handling it:

```
{{random_user_1}}: Hey Alice, what do you think about social media?
{{char}}: Social what, now? Is that a kind of tea party? I'm afraid I haven't heard of it.
```

The example does the heavy lifting. The negative guidance up top is reinforcement.

<Tip>
  You can't enforce hard rules through the Definition. If something *must* never happen, say, for safety, that's not what this is for. Sutorichat has platform-level moderation. Negative guidance is about *style*, not safety.
</Tip>
